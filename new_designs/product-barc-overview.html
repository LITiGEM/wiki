Barchitecture - what bacteria and architecture have in common

Bacteria can be engineered to generate sustainable and eco friendly building materials such as bricks, bioplastic products, items of clothing and “living materials” able to conduct electricity or emit light. Barchitecture is taking these developments a step forward and incorporate a light-control element to guide cell structuring into desired 3D structural arrangements.

Why use cells to generate structures?

  • cells span multiple length scales and can therefore be used to generate from microscopic to macroscopic structures
  • structures could self-heal and can be easily recycled by light-inducing cell death or polymer degradation
  • Allows the generation of structures in environments that humans have no direct access to or are life-endangering

Our vision is to create structure from living organisms. Given its complex nature, we decided to explore the implications of our project, steps needed to be taken towards this and how might one approach this challenge.

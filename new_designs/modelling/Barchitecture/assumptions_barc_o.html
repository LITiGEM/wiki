Cracking the Model

Methods and Assumptions

Mathematical Modelling describes complex biological system with mathematical equations and concepts. Setting up the initial conditions in the model is not that straight forward. If no experimental data is yet available, reasonable assumptions based on literature, logic and quantitative analysis need to be made. We believe that the initial assumptions made are crucial to crack the model. Once, established the parameters need to constantly be optimised with experimental data and/or literature data in order to fit reality to its maximum. Parameter optimisation adds all the wieght to a model's credibility, this is why we decidedto approach this in 2 ways: Sensitivity Analysis and Paramter Sampling.

Scroll down to find out more!

Sensitivity Analysis

Sensitivity Analysis is conventionally used for parameter optimisation. A range of independent variables are tested in this technique and their impact on the dependent variable is evaluated.

Method

  1. Identify the independent variables to be optimised
  2. Obtain ranges of values to be tested from literature
  3. Run the model and plot the graphs with the range of tested values and identify the optimal condition
  4. Re-run the initial model to check how the optimised parameters affected the rate limiting step, i.e. determine if the optimised step is prevailed to be the rate limiting step in the overall cellular mechanism
  5. Determine the areas in which research could be performed to optimise the parameters (Wet Lab contribution)

Parameter Sampling

Parameter sampling (ensemble modelling), is a method of parameter optimisation, which relies on the random sampling of parameter values within a specified range. A probability density function (PDF) determines the distribution of the continuous random variables evaluated in the function. Parameter sampling considers biological noise, making it a more realistic representation of the behaviour of a biological system.

Method

  1. Identify the parameters to be optimised
  2. Collect raw data from literature
  3. Process raw data by assigning weights to data depending on the similarity to the to-be-optimised parameter
  4. Generate probability densitiy functions (pdf); input the sample size, standard deviation and weighted mean

Assumptions

LEGIT MODEL

  1. Mass action kinetics
  2. Michaelis Menten kinetics
  3. All reactions are taking place in cells, which are in a petri dish
  4. Light intensity is the only factor affecting protein expression of the cells
  5. Every cell is activated by the same light intensity, regardless of their position in the petri dish
  6. There is no light reflection or refraction from the cells
  7. All cells are at their exponential growth phase
  8. All cells are equally as probable to express SpyTag and SpyCatcher
  9. SpyTag and SpyCatcher are constitutively expressed with intimin
  10. The amount of inactive EL222 transcription factors is equal to the amount of active EL222

LAME MODEL

  1. Mass action kinetics
  2. The reaction is taking place in a petri dish
  3. The same light intensity reaches every cell (thus they are all activated at the same time with the same light intensity)
  4. All cells are at their exponential growth phase
  5. PhoCl always cleaves when exposed to violet light (400 nm)
  6. LACE (transmembrane protein, photocleavable linker and the transcription factor) are constitutively expressed
  7. The cells are not going through the cell cycle when they are induced for the photocleavage of p65 (As during the cell cycle eukaryotic cells double the size of their nucleus)

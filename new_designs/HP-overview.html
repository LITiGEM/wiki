Light bulb prototyping - Artists save us

To make our light-bulb prototype look nice, we asked artists for designs. We reached 78,000 people, got 133 design submissions and developed our final prototype.

How we decided to go to Mars

We developed ideas with the European Space Agency, Transport for London and architects for biobricks to make, entrepreneurship and how to take bacterial architecture to Mars.

People dancing to transcribe proteins for LIT

We designed an interactive journey from DNA to 3D structures made out of bacteria. At London Science Museum’s Late event, 2000 guests played, learnt about our project and gave us feedback.

Organ printing - a mind-blow for policy

To develop policy for the future of organ printing, we talked to people at the Wellcome Trust Reading room about societal and ethical impacts of light-induced technologies.

Tonight's special: GMOs

EatoffTheMenu organize dinners on topics from AI to Trump politics. We collaborated to bring together 8 people to talk about our LIT project, its safety and whether we should have GMOs on our table.

iGEM UK debate who should own their projects

We organised debates at the UK iGEM meet-up to see how effective they are at revealing answers and producing more questions about synthetic biology.

Genetically modifying mules to make love

We talked to 17-year olds from UCL’s Bio Summer School about the impacts of modifying organisms on the environments they inhabit. The discussion led to modifying mules for agricultural purposes.

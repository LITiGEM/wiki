We envision a future where light will do more than illuminate your streets.

Synthetic biology will use light to control cellular processes and innovate sustainable living and medicine.

Our team has explored ways to combine biological light switches, either engineered or found in nature, into applications on Earth and beyond. The aim has been to standardise biological light switches and offer the iGEM community these tools together with an inspiration of how they can be used in the future of synthetic biology.

BIOLOGICAL LIGHT SWITCHES...HUH?

A biological light switch is any molecular machine that can activate or supress a cascade of cellular processes. Think of them as controls that turn your target outcomes on or off.

HOW WE EXPLORED THEM

The bulk of optogenetics research happens in neuroscience. However, we felt that optogenetic tools should become more popular in synthetic biology. We combined photosensitive proteins and photocaged non-natural amino acids into applications in medicine, architecture and illumination.

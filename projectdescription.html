What are they?

Light-sensitive proteins change their structures when light of specific wavelengths is absorbed. So, a cell might such a protein sitting on its surface. When light hits, it changes structure, triggering a cascade of events downstream.

Specific light wavelengths can break proteins apart. Think of it as light that makes 2 amino acids hate each other, thus splitting the protein into two. If that protein is blocking a process, light is therefore the perfect switch to turn it on.

There are such weird things as amino acids that are not natural. When they encounter light, they change similarly to what proteins change in response to light. For example, pyrolysine goes to lysine. I'll let you guess which one is the natural one.
